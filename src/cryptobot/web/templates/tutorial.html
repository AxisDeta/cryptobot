How To Use CryptoBot (Simple Guide)

This page explains every major section in plain language so both beginners and experienced traders can use the app confidently.

Back to App About Support Privacy

1. First Time Setup

  1. Create account and log in.
  2. Go to Billing + Activation.
  3. Choose a plan and pay.
  4. Use your activation key, then click Activate Key.

After activation, your subscription becomes active and the app unlocks predictions for your account/device.

2. Billing, Activation, and Subscriptions

Billing + Activation panel

This is where you buy/renew and activate access.

Activation Key

A unique code sent after payment. Enter it once to activate your license.

Your Subscriptions

This list shows your plan, status, key, activation time, and expiry date so you always know how much access time is left.

Manage Subscription

Opens billing when you want to renew or upgrade.

3. Signal Form (What To Fill)

  • Market: Crypto or Forex.
  • Exchange: For crypto only (Binance/Kraken/Coinbase).
  • Trading Pair: Example `BTC/USDT` or `EUR/USD`.
  • Timeframe: Example `1m`, `1h`, `1d`.
  • Risk Profile: Conservative, Moderate, Aggressive.

Then click Generate Signal.

4. What The Result Means

  • Current Decision: Buy, Sell, or Wait.
  • Up Probability: Chance price goes up.
  • Confidence: How strong the model believes this signal is.
  • Expected Volatility: How much price may move (higher means more risk).
  • Position Size: Suggested exposure size (not a guarantee).
  • AI Explanation: Human-style explanation of why the signal was produced.

If confidence is low, beginners should usually wait for a stronger setup.

5. What Is “Add To Watchlist”?

Watchlist helps you save pairs you monitor often.

  • Click Add to Watchlist after choosing a pair.
  • Saved items appear as quick chips.
  • Click a chip to instantly reload that pair into the form.

This saves time and reduces typing mistakes.

6. What Is “Recent Signals”?

Recent Signals is your personal log of generated signals.

  • It stores action, pair, timeframe, confidence, and time.
  • You can mark each as Win / Loss / Skipped / Pending.
  • This helps you review performance and improve decision discipline.

7. Understanding The Plots

Price chart (candles)

Each candle shows open, high, low, close during each period in your selected timeframe.

EMA lines

Smoothed trend lines. Useful for trend direction.

Model probability plot

Shows how model confidence in upward movement changes across the prediction window.

Chart Window options

  • Prediction Only: focus on forecast window.
  • Prediction + Last 24h: include recent context.

8. Beginner Mode

Beginner Mode hides technical clutter and shows plain-language action, strength, risk, and next step.

9. Easy Example

Example: It is 12:00 PM. You select `BTC/USDT`, timeframe `1h`, risk `Moderate`, then run signal.

  • Decision = WAIT
  • Up Probability = 42%
  • Confidence = 51%

Simple interpretation: setup is weak, so do not rush into a trade yet. Add BTC/USDT to watchlist and check again later when confidence improves.

10. Safety Rules

  • No model is 100% accurate.
  • Always use stop-loss.
  • Start with small size, especially in high volatility.
  • Do not trade based on one metric alone; combine action + confidence + volatility.
